The Ancient Craft of Vellum

💧
Maceratio

The raw hide is submerged in a bath of lime water, where it soaks for days. This alkaline solution loosens hair and dissolves unwanted proteins from the skin.

🪢
Tensio

The softened hide is lashed to a wooden frame with ropes and pegs. Careful, even tension is applied on all sides to begin transforming skin into a writing surface.

🔪
Rasura

A crescent-shaped lunellum knife is drawn across the stretched hide. Fat, flesh, and remaining hair are scraped away in long, steady strokes.

🌬
Siccatio

Still on the frame, the hide is left to dry slowly in open air. As moisture leaves, the skin tightens and becomes thin and translucent.

✨
Politio

A pumice stone is rubbed across the dried surface in gentle circles. This final polishing creates the smooth, silky finish that accepts ink beautifully.
